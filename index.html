I made this website with the intention of organizing all of my research and professional work into a single repository. My hope is that this site helps you get to know me as well as attain knowledge about the biologically inspired research that I have been involved in so far in my research career.

I have done a little bloging over the years, but now I have been inspired to accumulate all of my work both research and engineering into the blog below. In my blog I review a variety of biomedical research topics that have had major impacts on my research and academic education. I am writing with the hope of educating those outside of the scientific community who may be considering the field of biological engineering. What I have found is that most college students do not quite have an understanding of what Bioengineering actually entails. Most academic programs in bioengineering too often focus on applications of deep engineering concepts and not with enough diversity of biological concepts at the undergraduate level. My research experience has really helped me to define what Biologically inspired engineering looks like, especially for people who aspire to be engineers of the "Gene Era". We should aspire to employ engineering strategies to understand more diverse biological concepts than what may be taught to us in a classroom setting.
